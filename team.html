When you work with us, you get a dedicated team that values diversity. Team members range in age from 72 to 22, speak up to five languages, originate from three different countries, and have worked in four different industries before coming to ARSOME. We have found that our multicultural work environment has enriched our work and increased the satisfaction of our customers.

OUR STORY The Beginning

Benjamin contacted David to enroll in his Information Management course at Eastern Connecticut State University in 2015. It was at capacity, but David agreed to take on an extra student. It didn’t take long for Benjamin and David to realize their mutual passion for entrepreneurship and technology. Shortly after having completed his teaching assignment and having worked for seventeen years in Higher Education, David quit his job as an Instructional Design Manager and joined Benjamin to fulfill their entrepreneurial passion. Five years later, David and Benjamin continue to utilize technology as a solution to complex problems, while educating the community on the power of entrepreneurship.

OUR TEAM

Benjamin Williams

Co-Founder, Chief Executive Officer

He holds a B.S. in Business Information Systems from ECSU, an M.S. degree in Technology Management from NYU an MBA from UCONN and an M.A. from Harvard University. Prior to ARSOME, Benjamin was CEO of Innovative-Diffusion, a company that provided solar energy to communities in Africa, up until its successful exit in 2015. Ben is a certified Project Management Professional (PMP) and was inducted into Connecticut's 2019 40 under 40 class. He is a big time Boston Celtics fan and father to his silver lab, Celtics.

David Oyanadel

Co-Founder, Chief Innovation Officer

David was born in the beautiful country of Chile and once owned a walnut farm. He holds a B.S. degree in Computer Science from Eastern Connecticut State University, a M.S. degree in Instructional Design from Central Connecticut State University, and an EMBA from the University of Connecticut. Prior to this role, David was an Instructor and Manager of Instructional Design in CT's Higher Education network for 17+ years.

Mark E. Ojakian

Senior Advisor

Mark E. Ojakian served as President of the Connecticut State Colleges and Universities system in September 2015 until January 2021. He was chief executive for an organization responsible for 17 institutions of higher education, a $1.3 billion operating budget, 9,000 employees and over 100,000 students. Ninety six percent (96%) of the CSCU students are Connecticut residents. President Ojakian was also appointed by Governor Ned Lamont to lead Connecticut’s effort to reopen all public and independent institutions of higher education in the state during the COVID-19 pandemic and to serve on the Governor’s Workforce Council. Prior to his current appointment, he served as Chief of Staff to Governor Dannel P. Malloy for four years. He lives with his husband Jason Veretto in West Hartford. He has two children and four grandchildren.

Culture

Jeans and sweatshirts are typical attire in the office. Sandwiches can be found in the fridge, coffee pods in the upper right cabinet and slippers in the closet to the left. Our office is more than a workplace. It's a place to tell stories, solve problems, and collaborate with clients.

The culture is unique, without cubicles or competitiveness. Through successes and struggles, we always have fun in the process. Celtics, our Dog Therapist, named after that team in Boston, is there to pick us up when things are looking down. There's also Sam Adams, an old friend we may visit at the end of a long work week. Welcome to ARSOME.
